Why Raw?

Heart Rate Variability is one of the most revealing windows into your autonomic nervous system — a daily snapshot of how recovered, stressed, or activated you are at a physiological level.

Most HRV apps put an interpretation layer between you and that signal. You get a readiness score, a stress label, a color-coded verdict — whether the algorithm behind it is transparent or opaque.

Your readiness is 62. You are moderately stressed. Recovery recommended.

Maybe that's useful to you. Maybe it isn't. But either way, it's not the only option. HRVSpark skips the interpretation entirely and puts your raw numbers on your watch face — so you can watch the trends yourself, build your own intuition, and decide what the data means on your own terms.

The usual flow: data → opaque algorithm → verdict → user

HRVSpark: data → user → intuition

Design Philosophy

"I always drift up and vary a lot while sleeping. The numbers always crash after hot yoga. They go up with tvTENS on my ear. They go WAY up with a cold shower or ice plunge. I wanted to be able to see THAT — the actual shape of what's happening, not a number summarizing it."

— the creator

No interpretation means no color-coding by value. No anxiety means subtle aesthetics — dark backgrounds, soft blue glows, data that whispers rather than shouts.

Complication-first design means it's always there — not something you open, but something you glance at. Part of your watch face like the time and the date.

"The no-scores approach makes total sense. I found myself chasing the number instead of actually understanding my patterns. Removing the interpretation layer and just looking at the raw 7-day trend changed how I used the data completely. The anxiety that comes from a colored score or verdict is real. Raw numbers let you build your own intuition over time instead of outsourcing it to someone else's algorithm."

— beta tester

"[My data shown on HRVSpark is] interesting but probably predictable. My HRV is about double to triple when I'm at home versus when I'm at work. Must have a stressful job."

— a physician, beta tester

"Well done. Some of my patients who come in for yearly CPET testing seem to day-trade HRV numbers. They would love it."

— a sports medicine physician

Who It's For

The self-experimenter.

You're already tracking. You've already noticed patterns. You don't need an app to tell you what your body is doing — you need a better window to see it.

The biohacker.

You do cold exposure, breathwork, vagal nerve stimulation, HRV-guided training. You need granular, honest data to evaluate your interventions.

The HRV veteran.

You already use analysis tools. Maybe you love them, maybe you're ambivalent. Either way, you want something on your watch face that just shows the data — no scores to chase, no verdicts to react to.

The anxiety-conscious.

When the app stops telling you you're stressed, checking your HRV stops being a source of dread. It becomes simply informative.
