# Claims & Evidence Table

| # | Claim | Specific Metric / Evidence | Key Figure | Confidence |
|---|:------|:--------------------------|:-----------|:-----------|
| 1 | **The filament is a persistent defect of the forme, not transient debris.** | Present in all 55 examined copies from 23 institutions spanning 4 continents. No copy lacks the mark. | Fig. 1 (in situ crop), Fig. 2 (55-trace composite) | **High** — universal presence across independent copies. |
| 2 | **The filament's position drifted directionally during the print run.** | PC1 of resampled shape vectors explains 73.3% of total variance. Spearman $\rho$(PC1, centroid $x$) = 0.984, $p < 10^{-40}$. The drift is overwhelmingly horizontal and unidirectional. | Fig. 3 (PCA projection), Fig. 4 (gradient overlay) | **High** — statistically unambiguous monotonic trend. |
| 3 | **The filament's arc length increased along the drift axis.** | Spearman $\rho$(PC1, arc length) = 0.726, $p < 10^{-9}$. | Fig. 5 (feature trends) | **High** (metric). **Medium** (interpretation) — elongation could reflect physical stretching *or* the filament re-seating at a more oblique angle across the crop window. Uncropped-image analysis would disambiguate. |
| 4 | **Tortuosity ("wiggliness") did not change.** | Spearman $\rho$(PC1, tortuosity) = −0.064 (no trend). The filament's intrinsic shape complexity was invariant. | Fig. 5 (feature trends, bottom panel) | **High** — null result is itself informative: the filament moved and lengthened but did not buckle or kink. |
| 5 | **Printing occurred in stable bursts separated by discrete shifts.** | Ward hierarchical clustering at a 12-cluster cut yields one dominant cluster of 23 copies (42% of dataset, "Long Run," Cluster 9) with low internal variance (~15 px spread in $x_\mathrm{start}$). A secondary stable cluster of 6 copies (Cluster 10) is also present. Transitional singletons (e.g., 032, 042, 046) are geometrically distinct. | Fig. 6 (dendrogram), Fig. 7 (cluster scatter) | **High** — clustering convergence confirmed by both summary-statistics and full-shape methods. |
| 6 | **A candidate relative chronology can be reconstructed.** | Ordering by PC1 score yields a smooth progression: "early" copies (042, 034, 054, 001) are compact and left-shifted; "late" copies (038, 023, 026, 010, 006) are elongated and right-shifted. The Long Run occupies the middle ranks. | Fig. 4 (gradient overlay), Fig. 5 (feature trends) | **Medium** — the ordering is internally consistent but cannot be validated against an external absolute chronology. Relative ordering only. |
| 7 | **Chronology is relative, not absolute.** | No provenance data exists for the order of individual sheets within the 17th-century print run. Drying, gathering, and post-press handling introduce unknown reordering. | N/A (bibliographic logic) | **High** — fundamental limitation acknowledged. |
